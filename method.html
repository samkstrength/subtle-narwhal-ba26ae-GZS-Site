Why These Numbers Matter

The Standards 1.0 aren't about ego. They're about crossing the threshold from "I lift" to "I am strong." A 405×5 deadlift means you can handle serious physical work. A 315×5 squat means you've built real leg drive and structural integrity. A 225×5 bench and 135×5 press mean your upper body isn't a weak link. These numbers represent years of consistent training and the foundation for everything that comes after.

What Comes After

Once you hit the Standards 1.0, you don't stop training. You shift focus. You might pursue Standards 2.0 (higher weight benchmarks). You might add conditioning, sport-specific work, or hybrid training. You might maintain and focus on longevity. The point is you've built a base strong enough to support whatever comes next.

The Process

How we get you there.

Whether you're 25 or 75, starting from zero or chasing a big number, the system works the same way. Progressive, systematic, honest work.

01 — Learn the Lifts

We teach you the five core barbell movements with proper form. You learn how to squat, press, deadlift, bench, and clean safely and effectively. This phase is about building competency and confidence.

02 — Linear Progression

You add weight to the bar every session. This phase builds strength fast — often 5-10 pounds per workout on lower body lifts, 2.5-5 pounds on upper body. It doesn't last forever, but it works until it doesn't.

03 — Intermediate Programming

When you can no longer add weight every session, we shift to weekly progression. You're still getting stronger, just on a different timeline. This is where most people train for years — chasing the Standards or beyond.
